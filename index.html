🛡️ unissh - Connect to your servers securely now

Download for Windows

unissh helps you manage your remote computer servers. It keeps your passwords and keys safe in a vault. You store this vault on a server you control. Nobody can see your data except you.

🌟 What unissh does

Many SSH tools store your private keys in plain text on your hard drive. This exposes you to risk. unissh uses encryption to lock your data. You hold the key. We do not see your passwords. You own your security.

  • Privacy: Your data remains hidden.
  • Control: Host your own sync server.
  • Safety: Use deep encryption for all secrets.
  • Speed: Quickly jump between your saved servers.

💾 Getting setup

Follow these steps to run unissh on your Windows machine:

  1. Go to the official website to download the installer.
  2. Find the file ending in .exe in your Downloads folder.
  3. Double-click the file to start the installation.
  4. Follow the prompts on your screen.
  5. Open the unissh icon from your Desktop or Start menu.

The program will ask you to set up your vault. Pick a strong password. You will use this password every time you open the app to unlock your server details.

💻 System requirements

To use unissh, your computer needs:

  • Windows 10 or Windows 11.
  • A stable internet connection for syncing vaults.
  • Basic knowledge of your server IP address and username.

🔒 How security works

We use a system called zero-knowledge encryption. This means your vault stays scrambled until you enter your password. Even if someone steals your vault file, they cannot read it. Your password acts as the primary lock. Do not lose your password. We cannot reset it for you because we do not store it.

⚙️ Managing your tunnels

You can create tunnels to access private web interfaces on your servers. This allows you to view internal dashboards safely. The app handles the connection link automatically. Just select your saved server and click the Tunnel button.

🛠️ Support and help

If you face issues, check the help menu inside the app. Ensure your internet connection is active. If your vault fails to sync, check that your self-hosted server is running and reachable from your network.
